Contact Info

Name: Jinkyu Koo (구진규 in Korean; 具珍奎 in Hanja)
Address: 2788 San Tomas Expy, Santa Clara, CA 95051
Email: jinkyuk (at) nvidia (dot) com

I am now a senior deep learning software engineer at NVIDIA. I got my Ph.D. from Purdue University, studying issues on embedded systems, network security, and smart grid privacy, and have worked in industry as a software engineer on Linux systems, the physical layer of wireless communication systems, and machine learning accelerators. My current interest is accelerating deep learning applications using GPUs, covering TensorRT and CUDA issues.

I am a dad of two boys. As many would agree, parenting two boys is tough both mentally and physically. Every day, my wife and I are struggling to manage the right balance between work and parenting. However, I can say that the time spent with my kids is my best time of a day.

Throughout my childhood, I had learned that I am terrible at all kinds of sports that human beings created. However, in my mid-20s, I found out that there is an exception, which is an ice hockey. The ice hockey is the first sport that I felt I may be as good as others. I had played it for several years, sometimes seriously. Since my second son was born, I have stopped the ice hockey, but I always dream of resuming to play it in a near future.

Recently, I got a new hobby, which is tennis. I play tennis almost everyday. However, due to schedule conflicts, I do it mostly alone against a wall. I have played tennis with a human only a few times, so I do not really know if I am good or bad at it.

Since the shelter-in-place due to COVID19 began, I am also into backward farming. I grow multiple kinds of vegetables, but I am developing my specialty as germinating mango seeds. I am researching the most effective way to grow mango from seeds in California’s climate.
