I am a research scientist, working with Prof. Saurabh Bagchi in the Dependable Computing Systems Laboratory (DCSL) at Purdue University. I had worked for Samsung Electronics for about 7 years as a software engineer on Linux systems and as a research engineer on the physical layer of wireless communication systems. During my Ph.D., I studied issues on embedded systems, network security, and smart grid privacy. My current research interest is applying machine learning techniques to various application domains, which include:

  • Bioinformatics: deciphering microRNA regulatory interactions
  • Software engineering: symbolic execution, approximate computing


I am a dad of two boys. As many would agree, parenting two boys is tough both mentally and physically. Every day, my wife and I are struggling to manage the right balance between work and parenting. However, I can say that the time spent with my kids is my best time of a day.

Throughout my childhood, I had learned that I am terrible at all kinds of sports that humans created. However, in my mid-20s, I found out that there is an exception, which is an ice hockey. The ice hockey is the first sport that I felt I may be as good as others. I played it for several years, sometimes seriously. Since my second son was born, I have stopped the ice hockey, but I always dream of resuming to play it in a near future.
